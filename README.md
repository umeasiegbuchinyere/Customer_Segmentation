## Project Overview
This Customer Segmentation Dashboard provides a high-level analysis of customer purchasing behaviour using key metrics such as Revenue, Purchase Frequency,
Recency, and Purchase Value. The objective of the project is to help stakeholders understand how customers interact with the business, identify behavioural 
patterns, and support data-driven decision-making for customer retention and revenue growth.

## Data Source
The dataset used in this project is a sample retail customer transaction dataset created for data analytics and customer segmentation practice. 
It contains customer-level purchase information.

## Problem Statement
Businesses often struggle to identify which customers contribute the most value and which are at risk of becoming inactive. Without customer segmentation, 
marketing efforts become less effective and customer retention declines. This project uses customer purchase behaviour data to analyze revenue, recency, 
frequency, and purchase value, enabling stakeholders to identify customer patterns, optimize marketing strategies, and improve overall business performance.

## Insights From Analysis
1. Customer Base Overview
The analysis covers 300 customers, providing a comprehensive view of customer purchasing behaviour and overall business performance.

2. Revenue Distribution Varies Across Customers
Revenue is not evenly distributed across all customers, indicating that some customers contribute more to total revenue than others. This presents an
opportunity to identify and retain high-value customers.

3. Moderate Customer Purchase Frequency
The average purchase frequency is 12.69, suggesting that customers make repeat purchases. However, there is potential to further increase engagement
through loyalty initiatives and personalized marketing.

4. Customer Recency Highlights Re-engagement Opportunities
The average recency of 13.34 indicates that while many customers have made recent purchases, others may not have interacted with the business for some
time. These customers can be targeted with reactivation campaigns.

5. Average Purchase Value Provides Spending Benchmark
Customers spend an average of 11.25 per purchase, establishing a benchmark for evaluating the success of promotions, pricing strategies, and upselling
initiatives.

6. Customer Behaviour Differs Across Individuals
The dashboard reveals variations in purchase frequency, recency, and revenue among customers, demonstrating that different customer segments require
different engagement and retention strategies.

7. Interactive Dashboard Supports Customer-Level Analysis
Stakeholders can use the interactive customer filters to examine individual customer behaviour, making it easier to identify valuable customers,
investigate purchasing patterns, and support data-driven decision-making.

## Conclusion
The customer segmentation analysis provides valuable insights into customer purchasing behaviour by examining revenue, purchase frequency, recency, 
and purchase value. The dashboard highlights differences in customer engagement and spending patterns, enabling stakeholders to identify high-value 
customers, monitor customer activity, and make informed business decisions. Overall, the analysis supports the development of targeted strategies 
that can improve customer retention and drive sustainable revenue growth.

## Recommendations
- Focus on retaining high-value customers through loyalty and reward programs.

- Re-engage inactive customers with personalized marketing campaigns and special offers.

- Increase purchase frequency by implementing targeted promotions and cross-selling opportunities.

- Regularly monitor customer behaviour using the dashboard to identify trends and support data-driven decision-making.

- Continuously update customer segmentation to ensure marketing strategies remain relevant and effective.
